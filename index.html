Engines
R-2600
The R-2600 Twin Cyclone was a powerful engine used by the United States in World War 2. It was built using 14 cylinders arranged in 2 rows. It could produce up to 1,300 kW of power and it weighed approximately 2000 lbs. It was used on the F6F Hellcat, A-20 Havoc, B-25 Mitchell, TBF Avenger, SB2C Helldiver, and PBM Mariner.
Rolls-Royce Merlin
The Rolls-Royce Merlin is a British liquid-cooled V-12 piston aero engine. Its max capacity was 27 liters of fuel. It could produce up to 770 kW of power. It was used on the Fairey Battle, Hawker Hurricane, Supermarine Spitfire, and the Lancaster Bomber
Junkers Jumo 004
The Junker Jumo 004 was a breakthrough in aerial technology. It was the first ever turbo jet engine in operational use. Invented by German scientists, it could produce a up 17.7 kW per second. It was used on the Messerschmitt Me 262 fighter and the Arado Ar 234 reconnaissance/bomber.
The Davis Wing
The High Ratio Davis Wing was an American Wing primarily used on bombers. This wing was highly efficient, allowing a relatively high airspeed and long range. The wing was very thick, allowing better lift, but it had many tradeoffs. The wings were illequiped to handle bad weather and heaviar loads.
B-29 Superfortress
The B-29 Superfortress was one of the most powerfull bombers in World War 2. It was a four-engine propeller-driven heavy bomber designed by Boeing and boasted a production cost 1.7 billion dollars more than the Manhattan Project. It featured the best technology including massive defense cannons and the first-ever cabin pressure system in a bomber. It could carry 2,200 lbs of bombs, the most of any allied bomber.

Self-Sealing Fuel Tanks
Self-Sealing technology prevented fuel tanks from leaking fuel and igniting after being damaged by enemy fire. Preventing planes from catching fire increased the durability of the American Air Force significantly. Self-sealing tanks have multiple layers of rubber and reinforcing fabric, layer of vulcanized rubber, and layer of untreated natural rubber. When the fuel tank is hit, the fuel seeps into the layers, causing the untreated layer to swell.
